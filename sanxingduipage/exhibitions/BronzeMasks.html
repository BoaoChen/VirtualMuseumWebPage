3D Model: Vertical-eyed Bronze Masks

Historical Significance

Among the many bronze masks unearthed in Sanxingdui, the most peculiar and magnificent one is this bronze vertical-eyed mask, which is known as "clairvoyant eyes" and "superb ears". Its image features are: eyebrows pointed upward, eyes long and slanted, eyeballs extremely exaggerated, protruding forward in a columnar shape up to 16 cm; ears fully spread out to both sides; short nose bridge, nose wings curled upward and inward like a bull's nose; wide and deep mouth, deep and long upward seam, as if slightly revealing the tip of the tongue, in a mysterious smile. There is a square hole in the middle of its forehead, which may have been originally cast with exquisite forehead decorations. It can be imagined that its original overall image must have been even more exquisite and magnificent.

Dynasty

Chinese Historical Era, Shang (1600 BC ~ 1046 BC)

Shape Size

Length 138 cm, Width 85 cm, Height 66 cm.
