3D Model: Golden Sun Bird

Historical Significance

The whole vessel is round and the body is very thin. The pattern is hollowed out and divided into two layers, the inner layer is a circle with 12 tooth-shaped rays of light rotating clockwise at equal intervals; the outer layer pattern surrounds the inner layer and consists of four identical birds flying counterclockwise. The bird has a large head and claws, a long and thick neck and legs, a small body, short wings, a slightly hooked beak, a short drooping tail, and claws with three toes. The four birds are connected front and back, flying in the same direction, opposite to the direction of rotation of the inner vortex. The whole pattern is like a modern paper-cut work, with simple and smooth lines, rich rhythm, strong dynamics, and strong symbolic meaning and imagination. On August 16, 2005, the "Sun Bird" gold ornament pattern was announced by the State Administration of Cultural Heritage as the symbol of China's cultural heritage.

Dynasty

Chinese Historical Era, Shang (1100 BC ~ 771 BC)

Shape Size

Outer Diameter 12.53 cm, Inner Diameter 5.29 cm, Thickness 0.02 cm.
