3D Model: Shang Jade Ge Blade

Historical Significance

Zhang is one of the most important ritual vessels in ancient my country. It is believed to be an instrument used to "worship the south". It is generally believed that its main purpose is to offer sacrifices to mountains. This jade-edged Zhang unearthed from the No. 2 sacrificial pit is the most representative and exquisite cultural relic among the Sanxingdui jade artifacts. There are sacrificial patterns engraved on both sides of the body of the vessel, showing a solemn and grand sacrificial scene, providing precious information for the study of ancient Shu religious sacrificial rituals. From the analysis of the mountain in the picture, the Zhang inserted on the side of the mountain, and the people who are worshiping, it can be generally inferred that the picture shows the so-called "mountain tomb sacrifice" The grand sacrificial scene.

Dynasty

Chinese Historical Era, Shang (1600 BC ~ 1046 BC)
